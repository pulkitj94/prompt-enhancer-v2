Framework Reference

      
The 7 frameworks from the C40 Accelerator — applied to prompt engineering.

      

        
CC-SC-R — Professional Prompting
Context — Domain, audience, goal, data sources, brand voice.
Constraints — Policies, compliance, PII handling, tone boundaries.
Structure — Required sections, format, length, organization.
Checkpoints — Assumption flagging, risk identification, confidence levels.
Review — Human approval points, escalation triggers, accountability.

Every enhanced prompt is scored against these 5 dimensions.

        
RCT — Simple Prompting
Role — Who should the AI act as?
Context — Situation, audience, background.
Task — Specific output needed, format, constraints.

Use RCT for quick prompts. Switch to CC-SC-R for production-grade work.

        
SAC — AI Management Philosophy
Supervision — Humans oversee AI. AI is the intern, you are the manager.
Accountability — Human remains accountable. AI assists, humans decide.
Continuous Learning — Iterate prompts, review results, improve.

        
CREAM — Model Selection
C — Cost: Budget per query/month?
R — Response Time: Real-time or batch?
E — Expertise: General or specialized?
A — Accuracy: Cost of being wrong?
M — Modality: Text only, or images/audio/video?

        
MWAPA — Application Architecture
Model — AI brain. Prompt in, text out.
Workflow — Business logic, pre/post processing.
Agent — Autonomous decision-maker with tools.
Platform — Infrastructure, APIs, databases.
Application — User-facing product.

80% of AI product value lives outside the model.

        
Autonomy Levels — Agent Decision Framework
L1 Consultant — AI gives advice. You decide.
L2 Technician — AI executes a single task.
L3 Contractor — AI runs multi-step workflows.
L4 Team — Multiple agents coordinating.

Higher autonomy = stricter Constraints, Checkpoints, and Review.

        
Evals and Monitoring
Evals — "Did you build it right?" Pre-deployment. Pass/fail.
Monitoring — "Is it still working?" Post-deployment. Continuous.
Observability = Evals + Monitoring + Tracing.

The Observability tab tracks tokens, cost, latency, and traces every call.
